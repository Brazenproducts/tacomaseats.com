How to Install Tacoma Seat Covers

April 2026 · 7 min read

Good seat covers install without removing your seats. That said, doing it right takes some patience and a few tricks. We’ve installed covers on dozens of Tacomas and this is the process that gets you a clean, wrinkle-free fit every time.

What You’ll Need

  • Your seat covers (obviously)
  • A plastic trim removal tool or butter knife (for tucking)
  • 10-15 minutes per seat (front), 20-30 minutes (rear)
  • A warm day or warm garage (material stretches easier when warm)

Front Seat Installation

  1. Remove the headrest. Press the release button and pull it straight up. Set aside.
  2. Start with the backrest. Slip the cover over the top of the seatback like a pillowcase. Work it down evenly on both sides. Make sure the airbag seam (if applicable) aligns with the actual airbag location on the side of the seat.
  3. Secure the backrest straps. Most covers have elastic straps or hooks that attach underneath the seatback. Reach behind and pull them snug. The tighter, the better — slack creates wrinkles.
  4. Install the bottom cushion cover. Drape it over the bottom cushion and tuck the excess material into the gap between the seat base and the seatback. A plastic trim tool helps push fabric deep into the crevice.
  5. Secure bottom straps. Reach under the seat and hook/strap the bottom cover in place. Route straps around the seat rails, not through any mechanical components.
  6. Reinstall headrest. Push the headrest posts through the cover’s headrest holes. If the cover includes headrest covers, install those first.
  7. Smooth and adjust. Sit in the seat and adjust the cover. Pull out any wrinkles by tugging from underneath.
💡 Pro Tip: Leave the covers in direct sunlight for 30 minutes before installation. Warm Cordura and neoprene are more flexible and stretch over the seat contours more easily. Cold material fights you.

Rear Seat Installation

  1. Fold the rear seat forward (Double Cab). This gives you access to the mounting points underneath.
  2. Install the bottom cover first. Lay it over the bottom cushion and tuck straps underneath. The rear bottom is usually easier than the front because there are no rails to work around.
  3. Install the backrest cover. The Tacoma rear backrest folds forward, so the cover needs to accommodate this. Slip it over the top and work down. Secure straps behind.
  4. Check the 60/40 split. If your Tacoma has the 60/40 split rear (Double Cab), make sure each side is covered independently and the armrest/cup holder fold-down still works.
  5. Test fold-down functionality. Fold the rear seats forward and back to make sure covers don’t bind or bunch.

Common Mistakes

  • Airbag seam misalignment: If the cover’s airbag tear-away seam doesn’t line up with the actual airbag, reinstall. This is a safety issue.
  • Not tucking deep enough: The #1 cause of covers that look wrinkled or shift. Tuck aggressively into every crevice.
  • Loose straps: Straps should be tight enough that the cover doesn’t move when you sit down. Too loose = constant readjustment.
  • Installing on a dirty seat: Vacuum and clean your seats first. Dirt trapped under the cover can cause abrasion on the original upholstery.

Brand-Specific Notes

Bartact: Their covers use a hook-and-loop system that’s among the easiest to install. The included instructions are clear with diagrams for each Tacoma configuration. Average install time: 30-40 minutes for all seats.

Coverking: Made-to-order covers fit extremely precisely but can be tight to install initially. Use the warm-material trick. Once in place, the fit is unbeatable.

Universal covers: These are where most installation headaches happen. Expect excess material, imperfect strap placement, and compromises. Amazon reviews are your friend for universal cover install tips.
