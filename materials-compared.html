Seat Cover Materials Compared

April 2026 · 8 min read

The material your seat cover is made from determines everything: durability, comfort, water resistance, heat behavior, UV resistance, and price. Here’s an honest breakdown of every material you’ll encounter when shopping for Tacoma seat covers.

Cordura Nylon (Our Top Pick)

Cordura is the gold standard for tactical and heavy-duty seat covers. Originally developed by DuPont for military applications, 1000-denier Cordura is the same fabric used in military body armor carriers, backpacks, and combat gear. It resists abrasion, tearing, and UV degradation far better than any other seat cover material.

The trade-off is comfort. Cordura has a slightly rough texture compared to neoprene or leatherette. In hot weather, it can feel coarser against bare skin. But for Tacoma owners who wear work pants, jeans, or shorts with a towel (you know who you are), this isn’t an issue.

Best Cordura option for Tacoma: Bartact tactical covers — 1000D Cordura, vehicle-specific fit, MOLLE webbing, made in USA.

Neoprene

Neoprene is a synthetic rubber originally developed for wetsuits. It’s inherently waterproof, comfortable, and stretchy. For Tacoma owners who surf, kayak, mountain bike, or deal with wet conditions regularly, neoprene makes a lot of sense.

The downsides: neoprene doesn’t handle UV as well as Cordura (it degrades faster in direct sun), and it retains heat — hot neoprene on bare legs in summer is uncomfortable. It’s also not as abrasion-resistant, so heavy gear sliding across neoprene will show wear marks over time.

Best neoprene for Tacoma: Wet Okole (premium) or Rough Country (budget).

Canvas / Duck Cloth

Canvas is the traditional work-truck material. It’s tough, breathable (unlike neoprene), and has a utilitarian look that suits work trucks. Australian brands like Black Duck are known for premium canvas covers. It’s water-resistant but not waterproof — heavy rain will soak through. Best for dusty, dry-climate work trucks.

Leatherette / Faux Leather

If you want the look of leather without paying for Katzkin, leatherette covers can transform a cloth Tacoma interior. EKR makes some of the best custom-fit leatherette covers. The material wipes clean easily and looks sharp. But it deteriorates in UV faster than anything else on this list — expect cracking and peeling within 2-3 years if you park in direct sun.

Polyester / Budget Material

Generic polyester is what most sub-$100 seat covers use. It’s light, cheap, and provides minimal protection. UV fading happens fast, seams come apart under stress, and water resistance is limited. Only recommended as a temporary solution or for trucks you genuinely don’t care about.

Our Recommendation

For most Tacoma owners: Cordura if you use your truck hard, neoprene if comfort and water resistance are the priority. Skip canvas unless you’re specifically in a dry-climate work truck situation, and only go leatherette if you’re willing to replace covers every 2-3 years.
