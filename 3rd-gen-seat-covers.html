Best Seat Covers for 3rd Gen Tacoma (2016-2023)

April 2026 · 10 min read

The 3rd gen Tacoma brought a completely redesigned interior with different seat shapes, new side airbag placement, and (finally) a power driver’s seat on higher trims. All of this means you can’t just use 2nd gen covers and call it a day. You need covers specifically designed for the 2016-2023 platform. Here’s what works.

Critical Compatibility Note

Do not buy 2nd gen covers for a 3rd gen Tacoma. The seat shapes are different, the airbag locations are different, and the headrest mounting is different. Using the wrong covers can prevent airbag deployment, which is a safety hazard, not just a fit issue.

Best for Daily Drivers

If your Tacoma is primarily a daily driver and you want comfort over tactical features, Coverking’s Neosupreme is excellent. Custom-fit to the 3rd gen with proper airbag cutouts. The neoprene-like material is softer than Cordura, handles spills well, and doesn’t get scorching hot in direct sun like leather or vinyl. $280-380/row with 2-3 week lead time.

3rd Gen Specific Details

  • Power driver seat (2020+ TRD Off-Road/TRD Pro/Limited): The adjustment mechanism on the side means covers need a specific cutout. Bartact and Coverking handle this with separate SKUs. If a brand doesn’t mention power seat compatibility, assume it won’t fit.
  • TRD Pro headrests: Slightly different shape and embossing. Most covers fit over them, but the Bartact covers have a specific TRD Pro headrest option that fits flush.
  • Heated seats: Available on Limited and TRD Pro. Thick Cordura can reduce heat transfer by 10-15%. Neoprene (Wet Okole, Coverking) transmits heat better.
  • Rear center armrest: The 3rd gen Double Cab has a fold-down center armrest. Good covers include an armrest cover; cheap ones don’t.
