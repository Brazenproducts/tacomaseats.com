Installation Tips

Most tactical seat covers can be installed in 30–45 minutes per seat. Here are our quick tips:

  • Remove headrests first — it makes getting the cover over the top MUCH easier.
  • Work from the top down — stretch the cover over the headrest area, then work down to the base.
  • Tuck firmly — use a plastic trim tool to push fabric into the seat crease between cushion and backrest.
  • Connect straps underneath — most covers use hook-and-loop or buckle straps under the seat. Make sure these are snug.
  • 3rd gen: disconnect battery — when working around side airbag areas, disconnect the battery for 30 seconds first as a safety precaution.

Material Comparison

Not sure what material is right for your use case? Here's the quick breakdown:

  • 1000D Cordura (Bartact) — Military-grade, maximum abrasion resistance, lasts 5+ years. Best for trail/work trucks.
  • 500D Cordura (Coverking) — Lighter but still durable. Good for daily drivers who want quality.
  • Polyester 600–900D (Smittybilt, Razorback) — Cheaper, fades faster in UV, pills over time. Fine for 1–2 years.
  • Neoprene (Diver Down) — Water-resistant, comfortable, but traps heat and less abrasion-resistant. Best for water sports.
