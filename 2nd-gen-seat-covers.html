Best Seat Covers for 2nd Gen Tacoma (2005-2015)

April 2026 · 11 min read

If you’re still driving a 2nd gen Tacoma, you’re in good company. These trucks are legendary for reliability, and plenty of them are rolling past 250,000 miles with the original drivetrain. The interior, though? That’s a different story. After 10-20 years, even well-maintained cloth seats show their age — stains set in, foam compresses, and the bolsters wear down from years of climbing in and out.

Seat covers are the smartest investment you can make for a 2nd gen. They either preserve resale value (Tacomas still fetch insane money used) or cover up existing wear so the interior feels fresh again. Here’s what we recommend after testing covers on our own 2008 and 2014 Tacomas.

Our #1 Pick: Bartact Tactical

Best Overall

Bartact 2nd Gen Tacoma Covers

Bartact makes separate covers for 2005-2008 and 2009-2015 2nd gen Tacomas because the seats changed slightly between those year ranges. This kind of attention to detail is why they’re our top pick. The 1000D Cordura nylon panels on the seat face handle decades of abuse, the UV-protected polyester on the sides prevents fading even with windows down all summer, and the MOLLE webbing on the seatbacks is genuinely useful for overlanders and hunters.

Coverking builds each cover to order for your exact year, model, and trim. Their Cordura Ballistic line uses genuine Cordura (not a knock-off) and the fit is OEM-level precise. Best Budget

Rough Country Neoprene

If you want basic water-resistant protection without the tactical price tag, Rough Country’s neoprene covers are a solid choice. They fit the 2nd gen well, they’re comfortable (neoprene feels better than Cordura in hot weather), and they’re about half the price of Bartact. Not as durable long-term, but great for the truck that sees occasional weekend adventures. $180-250/row.

Cheapest Decent Option

FH Group Universal

We normally don’t recommend universal covers. But for a high-mileage 2nd gen where you just need something to cover up existing damage, FH Group makes serviceable universal covers for $40-60. The fit won’t be perfect, they’ll shift around a bit, and they’ll wear out in 12-18 months. But at that price, you can replace them annually without blinking.

2nd Gen Tacoma Seat Cover Tips

  • Access Cab jump seats: The fold-up rear seats in the Access Cab are a weird shape. Most brands only make covers for the Double Cab rear. Bartact is one of the few that offers Access Cab-specific rear covers.
  • Pre-runner seats: If you have the Pre-Runner or TRD Off-Road with the taller bolsters, make sure your covers accommodate the extra bulk. Bartact fits these correctly; universal covers usually don’t.
  • Seat heater compatibility: Some 2nd gens have heated seats (mainly Limited trim). Thick covers can reduce heat transfer. Bartact and Coverking confirm compatibility; thicker neoprene may noticeably reduce heat output.
